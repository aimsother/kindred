She Was One Paycheck Away From Losing Her Kids — Then a Millionaire Called

Divorced, raising two kids alone, one child seriously ill. A desperate 24-hour shift brought police and child services to her door. Then a stranger offered something she never expected.

Toronto single mother Elena (name changed for privacy) knows what rock bottom looks like. Divorced, living alone with two children — seven-year-old Alice and five-year-old Kirill, who suffers from severe asthma — she had been drowning for months. Her admin job paid $2,000 a month. Rent ate half. Medication took most of what was left. Food was an afterthought.

“I stopped eating so my kids could have meat,” she recalls quietly. “But when Kirill had a bad week and I missed work, they let me go. That’s when everything fell apart.”

Desperate and out of options, she took a 24-hour warehouse shift for $70. She fed the kids dinner, kissed them goodnight, and promised to be back by morning. But around midnight, Kirill couldn’t breathe. Alice panicked. Both children screamed for hours. Neighbors called the police.

“The police were at my door at 6 a.m. My kids were terrified, the inhaler was dead. Child services gave me a final warning: find stable income and hire a caregiver — or lose my children.”
